Church WordPress Help & Support

Practical WordPress support for church websites that are broken, outdated, or hard to manage. If you're not sure what's wrong—or you're not sure who has access—I can help you stabilize the site and decide on the next step.

Get Help With Your WordPress Site Common Issues

Common WordPress Situations I Help With

If any of these sound familiar, you're not alone. Church WordPress sites often become fragile when responsibilities change or maintenance gets deferred.

Updates feel risky

WordPress, plugins, or themes are overdue, and you're worried an update will break the site.

The site is slow or unstable

Errors, downtime, or performance issues make the site feel unreliable.

No one knows who has access

Hosting, domain, admin accounts, and vendor services aren't clearly owned or documented.

Forms or giving stopped working

Contact forms, event registrations, or donations stopped working or became unreliable.

Volunteers are afraid to touch it

The system feels fragile, confusing, or inconsistent—so content updates get avoided.

Pages and features are unreliable

Content or features appear, disappear, or load inconsistently, making the site hard to trust.

How I Help

The goal is to restore stability quickly, then create clarity so the site doesn't keep returning to the same problems.

Step 1

Stabilize first

Fix what's broken and reduce immediate risk. Calm triage before big decisions.

Step 2

Clarify what you have

Identify what systems are in play (hosting, domain, plugins, vendors) and who owns what.

Step 3

Recommend next steps

You get a clear recommendation in plain language—support, improvement, or (sometimes) a rebuild.

You don't need to know the right terminology

If you can describe what you're seeing and what outcome you need, that's enough. I'll ask a few clarifying questions and propose a sensible next step.

What This Is (and Isn't)

This isn't a quick redesign pitch. It's practical help to get your WordPress site stable, understandable, and supportable over time.

This is

Calm triage when something is broken
Clear recommendations in plain language
Ongoing support when your church needs it

This isn't

Pressure to rebuild before we understand the problem
A one-off "fix" with no documentation
A vague handoff to "figure it out later"
