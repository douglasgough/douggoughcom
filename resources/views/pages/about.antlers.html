About Doug Gough

I help churches and Christian organizations keep their websites stable, clear, and well cared for over time. A website that supports ministry should be treated as a long-term responsibility—not a series of disconnected projects.

A Focused Way of Working

Many web teams are structured around a steady flow of new projects. That model can work well, but it often leaves less room for long-term care once a site is launched.

My work is intentionally structured around continuity. Whether working independently or as part of a larger team, my focus is on ongoing support—the steady work that keeps a church website healthy over time.

Continuity

Predictability

Sustained care

Ongoing Stewardship for Church Websites

Church websites support communication, community life, and mission. They also sit in the middle of church leadership, volunteer capacity, and limited time.

That's why I prioritize stability and clear decision-making—so the website serves ministry instead of becoming a recurring source of risk or distraction.

Stability and risk reduction

Updates, backups, and security handled responsibly so problems don't pile up.

Clarity and continuity

Clear responsibility and documentation so continuity doesn't depend on one person.

Practical improvement

Incremental changes that improve clarity and usability without unnecessary disruption.

New Work Usually Grows Out of Existing Relationships

I do build new websites and new features. But in most cases, that work grows out of an existing relationship—once there's trust, context, and shared priorities.

That approach tends to produce better outcomes: predictability, better decisions, less rework, and a site that stays sustainable after launch.

When a rebuild is needed

Sometimes the most responsible next step is a rebuild—especially when the foundation is no longer safe or maintainable. When that's the case, I'll explain why, and what the options are.

When improvement is better

Often the best next step is stabilization and incremental improvement—so you gain reliability and clarity without disruption.
