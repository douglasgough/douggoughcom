How pricing is approached

I work as a seasoned professional providing long-term website stewardship. That means you're paying for judgment and prevention, not just task execution. Issues are handled before they become emergencies, and decisions are made with continuity in mind.

  • Risk reduction through careful updates, backups, and safe change management.
  • Predictability through defined scope and a steady cadence of care.
  • Continuity through documentation and consistent ownership of the work.

A simple overage rule

If work exceeds plan hours in a given month, additional time is billed at $105/hr, with approval before any significant overage.

(If you'd rather cap work instead of overage billing, we can do that too.)

A note on affordability and stewardship

Not every church has the same financial capacity—and that's understood. At the same time, sustainable care requires sustainable pricing. Under-pricing ongoing responsibility ultimately helps no one.

Each year, I reserve limited space for reduced-rate engagements where the fit is right and expectations are clear. These are handled intentionally, not by default. If budget is a concern, we can talk honestly about scope and options.

Common questions

A few practical details that help set expectations and protect predictability on both sides.

Do plan hours roll over?

Typically no—monthly plans are priced around a steady cadence. If you expect uneven demand, we can choose a plan with more headroom or use the overage rule for occasional spikes.

What if we need a bigger project?

Larger efforts (rebuilds, redesigns, major migrations) are scoped separately. Ongoing plans are ideal for steady stewardship and safe evolution, not indefinite major project work.

Do you work with WordPress only?

WordPress is common, but not exclusive. If your site is on another platform, I'll tell you quickly whether I can support it well.
