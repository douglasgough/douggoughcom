How I Work

If you need help now, I can step in quickly. This page explains what to expect when we work together—how we start, how work moves forward, and how decisions get made.

Fast stabilization

If something is broken, the first priority is to restore stability and reduce risk.

Plain-language decisions

You'll understand what I'm recommending, why I'm recommending it, and what it affects.

Steady follow-through

Work is documented as we go, so progress doesn't disappear when people change.

What Happens First

The first goal is to get oriented quickly, reduce risk, and establish a clear plan.

Step 1

Quick intake

A short call or message thread to understand the situation, what's urgent, and what "done" means.

Step 2

Access and visibility

I gather access (hosting, domain, CMS, analytics) and confirm what systems are actually in play.

Step 3

Stabilize immediate issues

If something is broken, I focus on restoring function and reducing immediate risk first.

Step 4

A clear next-step plan

You get a short list of recommended actions, in order, with rationale and tradeoffs.

How Work Moves Forward

A simple workflow keeps things moving without confusion.

Communication

Clear updates in plain language
Decisions documented as we go
Predictable outcomes

Decisions

A clear next step agreed
Tradeoffs made explicit
Priorities confirmed before work begins

Implementation

Make small, safe changes first
Test and verify each change
Rollback quickly when needed

What You Get

A website that's brought back under control, plus the clarity needed to move forward responsibly.

A stabilized, up-to-date website

Immediate issues are addressed, risk is reduced, and the site is brought back to a reliable baseline.

A clear roadmap for what comes next

You get a short, prioritized plan for improvements—so future work is deliberate, not reactive.

Clear records and documentation

Decisions, changes, and system details are recorded so context isn't lost over time.

What I Need From You

A few basics make everything smoother and faster.

Required

Access

Hosting, domain registrar, CMS admin, and any third-party services your site depends on.

Required

One point of contact

A single person to confirm priorities and keep decisions moving.

Required

A clear goal

What "success" looks like for the next step—stability, clarity, improvements, or support.

Required

A simple written agreement

A short agreement that clarifies scope, timelines, and responsibilities—so work stays clear and decisions are documented.
