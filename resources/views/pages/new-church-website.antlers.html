New Church Websites

I build new church websites when a rebuild is truly the right next step—especially when the current site is unstable, outdated, or no longer serving the church well. The goal isn't just a new look. It's a site that is reliable, understandable, and supported over time.

When a New Website Makes Sense

A rebuild is usually the right move when the current site can't be stabilized without ongoing fragility or risk.

The foundation is unstable

Updates, plugins, or hosting issues keep causing breakdowns, and the site can't be trusted.

The platform is outdated

The current system is difficult to maintain, poorly supported, or locked into old assumptions.

Structure needs a reset

Navigation and content have grown confusing over time, and visitors can't find what they need.

The church has changed

Ministry priorities, programs, or communication patterns have shifted significantly since the site was built.

Content needs careful migration

Sermons, events, ministries, and resources need to be carried forward without losing history or context.

You need a clean handoff

The new site must be understandable for staff and volunteers, with structure and documentation.

When Improvement May Be Enough

In many cases, a church doesn't need to rebuild from scratch. Stabilizing, simplifying, and clarifying responsibilities can solve the real problems.

The site is workable, but neglected

Updates, backups, security, and basic care may be missing—creating risk that looks like "we need a new site."

A few targeted changes would help

Navigation cleanup, content restructuring, and dependable maintenance often restores confidence without a full rebuild.

You'll get a clear recommendation

If you contact me about a new website, I'll tell you plainly whether a rebuild is the best next step—or whether stabilization and improvements are likely to meet your needs.

How New Websites Are Approached

A rebuild should reduce risk, clarify responsibilities, and produce a site that your church can maintain with confidence.

Clear scope and priorities

You'll know what we're doing, what we're not doing, and what a successful launch looks like.

Structure and content first

We organize information so visitors can find what they need. Visual design supports the structure—not the other way around.

Predictable launch and handoff

The launch is planned carefully, and the result is understandable for staff and volunteers.

Maintainable by volunteers

The editing experience is simplified so your team can keep the site current without fear.

Documentation and ownership

Roles, responsibilities, and key decisions are recorded so knowledge doesn't disappear when people change.

Ongoing support after launch

A new website isn't the end of the story. I offer ongoing support so the site remains reliable over time.
